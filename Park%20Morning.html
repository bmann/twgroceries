Took our chairs to the park and got coffee and chai from Coho Coffee, plus a croissant and an ube babka.

Reading my Cook's Illustrated May & June 2021 and looking through the articles and recipes.

Hot-smoked Salmon Kedgeree, a Scottish-style kedgeree. Reading the intro makes me think a lot about colonial cultures, that part about where spice comes back in because Britain has many people of South Indian descent as part of their culture now. Should we be celebrating colonial cooking culture like this?

From the intro to Hot-smoked Salmon Kedgeree:

Along with Major Grey’s chutney and mulligatawny, kedgeree is a product of the Raj, the period between 1858 and 1947 when the British occupied and ruled the Indian subcontinent. Indians has been eating khichri (or khichdi), a spiced mixture of rice and lentils or mung beans, for centuries. The British kept the rice but removed the spices and substituted more familiar ingredients such as boiled eggs and smoked fish for the pulses, creating a wholly different dish with a similar name. Smoked haddock is traditional, and in recent years, spices such as turmeric, curry powder, and coriander have found their way back into British kedgeree.

After the park we headed out to Deep Cove to the Seymour Art Gallery to pick up Rachael’s art from a show there.

On the way back, going along the Dollarton Highway, we drove past The Crab Shop. We ordered fish & chips for lunch and also some prawns for dinner, sourced wild from the Sea of Cortez.

For dinner, cooked the prawns in sautéed onions and garlic with butter and olive oil. Finished off with fresh lemon juice and a little maple syrup.

Yes, I should have shelled them before cooking.

Served with egg noodles - Anna’s Country Kitchen Extra Broad Noodles - and a little salad of cucumbers and cherry tomatoes.

For Monday, I prepped the chicken drumsticks and chicken wings with a marinade. First I cut the wings in half to make wing and drumette with my poultry shears.

Then, in a bowl, mixed them together with:

  • grapeseed oil
  • onion granules
  • garlic granules
  • mustard powder
  • cumin, ground in the mortar & pestle
  • paprika, lots of it
  • salt
  • red wine vinegar
  • cayenne powder
