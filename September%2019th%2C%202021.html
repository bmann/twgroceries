A bunch of images and activities to document from the week.

We just got back from a neighbourhood walk. Rachael likes to make sure she gets 10K steps in per day and it was looking like rain so we headed out after breakfast.

No idea what these strange fruits are. They kind of look like pods, but they are more like fused together petals or lobes. Research needed!

There are lots of Pacific dogwood trees in our neighbourhood, and they are filled with berries. They are "edible but not palatable" according to descriptions. I tried some, and they are currently very soft and mushy and filled with seeds, so not very appealing to eat. The flavour is nice and floral and sweet. I'd almost say the closest to a hint of guava flavour that I've come across. Would probably make nice jam, or rather jelly to strain out all the seeds.

There are lots of homes in our East Van area with well tended vegetable gardens. Saw these melons in a front yard:

May be some sort of gourd or bitter melon? There were two more that had slings around them, and then some more gourd shaped ones that were a different kind, maybe the Chinatown Melons Image that might have been calabash?

Yesterday we went to the Harvst Market at Strange Fellows Brewing. It's organized by our friend Grace of eikcam ceramics.

Here are some of the daruma she makes.

We had some treats from Subo, a Filipino baker. A peach mango hand pie, a slice of bibingka, and one more square that was rice pudding and condensed milk that I didn't get the name of.

I tried the canned wine from Strange Fellows. I tried the white, they also have rose and red. It was tasty "table wine", but at $28 for four pack, it just doesn't make sense to me. Wine is so expensive in BC!

We also met Mariko Ando, an artist who makes prints often featuring fantastical rabbit creatures.

And earlier in the week:

Cardamom Tepache September 2021

Other than skin and core of one fresh pineapple, this was 3" knob of fresh ginger, sliced, ~1/2 cup of cane sugar, 2 Tbsp of leftover maple syrup, and a handful of green cardamom pods that I mashed in the mortar and pestle.

Bought this drink at Coho Cafe, as well as trying out more things from the new menu. Specifically, the Brazilian waffles which are made by Beiju Foods out of cassava flour and cheese. A little too chewy for me.

VanSuya Hibis Berry Drink

It's a Nigerian specialty, known as Zobo, or Hibiscus tea, with pineapple, orange and spices added. The drink tasted too strongly of cloves for me.
