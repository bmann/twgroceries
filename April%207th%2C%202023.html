Over on Bowen Island for a couple of days over Easter.

Had some leftover Rouladen along with some Roh gebratene Kartoffeln.

Went for the traditional cappuccino at Tell Your Friends Cafe down on the dock on Friday when it was gloriously sunny in the afternoon.

No ham here on Saturday night, but I did make a pork roast. Seared it on all sides, spiced with salt, black pepper, ground coriander, and paprika. Slathered it in miso, and added butternut squash, onions, and carrots to the bottom of the pan with a little water. Lid on, cooked in the oven for 2 hours. Came out nice and tender.

My mom had some bok choy and a simple recipe for it. She writes it up on a little kitchen whiteboard to make it easy to read.

We subbed some miso paste and a little water instead of mushroom sauce.

Stir fried bok choy with garlic

  • 1 lb bok choy
  • 4 tsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Chinese mushroom sauce
  • sesame seeds
  • salt & pepper
  • red pepper flakes

You’re supposed to blanche the bok choy in boiling water for 2 minutes and then put in an ice bath before stir frying.

Heat oil in skillet, add garlic cloves, stir fry 30 seconds until fragrant.

Add bok choy, toss to coat, cook for 2-3 minutes until starting to brown. Add sauces and stir & coat for 1 minute, add spices.
