Salep

aka Turkish Hot Milk With Cinnamon

Turkish salep (sah-LEP) is a rich, hot beverage made with hot milk and sugar that is thickened with flour made from wild orchid tubers.

In Turkey, the beautiful orchid grows wild in forests, meadows, and even on the roadside. It's prized not only for the flower trade but also as one of the key ingredients in salep.

Once the orchid tubers are harvested, they are washed, boiled, dried, and then pounded into a fine flour that gives the drink a unique, earthy flavor and creamy texture.
