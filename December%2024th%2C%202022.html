Christmas Eve!

We’re having Katie & Brooke over. They’re making schnitzel and pavlova, we’ve got spaetzle and brussel sprouts planned.

(Yes I posted this at like 2am)

Went out with Rachael in the morning to the library and to pick up a few things at Dilly Dally.

I needed to pick up pork for schnitzel but Pasture to Plate didn’t open until noon. I hung out on the front porch of Livia with a coffee while waiting.

I had the butcher cut thin slices of boneless pork loin. Also bought a pork leg steak and some thinly sliced beef heart.

Grabbed lox next door from Daily Catch Seafood, then on to Santa Barbara.

1:30pm

Put the Rooster Sweetened Condensed Milk can in a pot of water to make dulce de leche. Opened the lid and looked at this Garlic & Zest how-to.

Prepping a pot of sauerkraut, garlic coil sausage, and a pork leg steak. I trimmed the fat cap and putting it on top.

Prepping the pork loin for schnitzel. They are small and have a big fat cap so the fat is also going on top of the sauerkraut pot.

I flattened the pork by covering it in a big ziploc bag and using a small wooden cutting board on top.

3:15pm

The sauerkraut pot looks amazing, the fat on top browning.

I forgot to top up the water for the condensed milk. Did that and turned it up slightly.

I have sliced 6 onions and put them in a pot with some of the pork fat cap rendered down. Added 1/3 of a cup of butter and some salt and simmering down.

Prepped the brussel sprouts by halving / quartering. Diced 3 slices of bacon — that will be the base for the sprouts — and set aside in a small bowl in the fridge.

Now going to make spaetzle batter.

Later

Time passes and the evening is over.

Schnitzel was egg, flour, and home made breadcrumbs by Brooklyn.

Brussel sprouts were bacon, pepper, lemon juice, and finished with a little chicken stock and sherry vinegar.

The pork sauerkraut pot was deliciously melted together. The table called it “meat trifle”.

The onions were onion gravy … sort of. I looked up a Spruce Eats Onion Gravy recipe and then just winged it. Added some Harvey’s Bristol Cream sherry, some American saffron, and some chicken stock. It was very good with the schnitzel and on spaetzle.

Katie made meringue (pavlova) and we had whipped cream. Covered half in sliced bananas and duce de ledge, and half in sugared berries.

Notes

We had a bit of lox with capers, red onions, and cream cheese on toasted foccacia squares as an apple. Very good lox from Daily Catch Seafood. The foccacia squares were also good with onion gravy.
