Last night I took out a frozen boneless leg of lamb and let it thaw on the counter for a bit. I unwrapped it, including the mesh holding the leg together, and covered it in a dry rub of whole cumin seeds, garlic flakes, salt, and onion granules. Put it in the fridge overnight.

This morning I put it in a sous vide for about 7 hours at 55C.

I was thinking cumin lamb and Sichuan peppercorn, to try out the new ones I bought yesterday.

Here are some research links:

  • Real-Deal Xinjiang Cumin Lamb (孜然羊肉) https://omnivorescookbook.com/cumin-lamb/
  • Spicy Sichuan-Style Lamb with Cumin https://www.foodandwine.com/recipes/spicy-sichuan-style-lamb-cumin
  • Szechuan Pepper Lamb https://www.cookitsimply.com/recipes/szechuan-pepper-lamb/
  • Xinjiang Cumin Lamb https://thewoksoflife.com/cumin-lamb/

The lamb was perfectly done already when it came out of the sous vide. I sliced up half of it and put it away for leftover lamb made another way. Or maybe just with mustard on some good bread!

To make a sort-of cumin lamb with Sichuan peppercorns was the goal for dinner. I trimmed fat off and put it in a pan with some oil to render. I cut the lamb into rough chunks and slices. To the oil I added 2 Tbsp of Sichuan peppercorns, and scraped off the cumin and garlic flakes from the sous vide lamb and put in the oil. Also added a Tbsp of finely diced ginger.

I roughly chopped an onion and added it after the spices had toasted a bit, and turned down the heat. Sautéed until soft.

I added a Tbsp of sesame oil and turned up the heat again. Added in the lamb and sautéed it a bit. Then added juices from the sous vide bag, 2 Tbsp of rice vinegar and 2 Tbsp of soy sauce. Added in 2 chopped bell peppers, one red and one yellow. Crumbled in one dried chilli, shaking out some of the seeds.

Put it under the broiler in the oven on high for 10 minutes.

Served it over rice. For my taste could have been more peppercorns and more chilies, but it was very tasty.

The Sichuan peppercorns were more floral than ones I’ve gotten, but I feel like a couple of places like Nine Dishes where I’ve had them have really numbed me.
