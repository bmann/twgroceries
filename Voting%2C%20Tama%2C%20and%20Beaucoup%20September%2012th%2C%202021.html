Voting, Tama, and Beaucoup September 12th, 2021

Boris Mann 12th September 2021 at 6:33pm
JournalShopping Trip

We went over to Hastings at Renfrew to do our early voting today.

Rachael had noticed Tama Organic Life, a grocery store across the street, so we checked it out.

Tama First Visit

Tama Organic Life is a Japanese corner grocery store. They have lots of fresh fruit and vegetables and direct connections to a variety of farms, as well as a variety of organic products, including some that are clearly imports from Japan.

They package various bulk items into smaller amounts, and also make their own dried goods and other products.

They make a vegan bento box and other items. Those pickled things in the background are a kind of Japanese Shallot apparently. Also frozen prepared food in the freezer, like the kabocha croquettes we bought.

Behind the register, they list the farms they work with:

  • Parsons Farm, Keremeos, BC
  • Umi Nami Farm, Victoria, BC
  • Amazia Farm, Oliver, BC
  • Yoshi's Farm
  • Amandah's Farm, Lytton, BC

Here's what we ended up buying:

  • carrot
  • dried mango
  • heirloom tomato
  • kabocha croquettes
  • shisito pepper
  • Kikkoman Organic Soy Sauce
  • Healthy Elements Handmade Fermented Wide Noodles

Then we went over to Beaucoup Bakery to pick up our order of mooncakes.

Beaucoup Mooncake Unboxing

We pre-ordered mooncakes from Beacoup Bakery for the Mid-Autumn Festival they called La Petite Lune – Scout Magazine covered it.

These are smaller mooncakes, featuring brown butter sweet potato with roasted pecan marzipan; vanilla rice pudding with pistachio marzipan; and raspberry currant currant cheesecake.

The packaging was all beautiful.

These are called The Prince (mango sago pomelo) and The Fox (jasmine strawberry) and are 3.25" tarts for sharing :)
