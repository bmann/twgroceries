Nikkei & Drinks September 5th, 2021

Boris Mann 11th September 2021 at 4:59am
Journal

Rachael had a morning catch up with friends in Woodland Park that I helped carry out our Woods camping chairs for, and then relaxed at home playing video games for a bit.

Roland and Barb picked us up before noon to go out to the Nikkei Museum, which was having a festival, the Nikkei Matsuri.

Turns out matsuri means festival, and there is a good write up on the Nikkei Matsuri website explaining the background. It's part Shinto / Buddhist religious festival, and part community celebration.

There were food stalls, vendors selling clothing and other goods, and a selection of food trucks parked on the outside.

  • oden - one pot dish of things cooked in a soy + dashi broth
  • takoyaki - dough balls filled with seafood, in this case some with shrimp and some with squid
  • tayaki - fish-shaped waffle (not pictured)
  • gyoza
  • rice "burger" with beef sukiyaki
  • Tokyo Katsu Sand food truck - rare beef and pork katsu sandwiches (not pictured)

Plus a selection of desserts, mostly filled with red bean paste.

Close up of oden, with fish ball, yam, turnip, tofu and hard boiled egg in a soy dashi broth. The paste is home made wasabi – no preservatives so it's brown rather than green.

Looks like a farmers market is ongoing until October, so we might be back to check that out:

Once we were done eating and shopping, we went next door to the little grocery store, Suzuya.

We bought some rice snacks that we had at the Kitoki Inn on Bowen, some white miso Hikari Miso Savoury White Miso, and some umeboshi gummy candies.

That was kind of a lot, but when we got home in the afternoon I ended up heading out to meet Yang and Ryan for Drinks at MILA September 5th, 2021.
