Art galleries and new cookbooks

Boris Mann 7th June 2021 at 7:28am
Journal

Saturday, June 5th, 2021

On Saturday, we went to the Vancouver Art Gallery. The show was just OK, at best. But this full wall mural by Lauren Brevner and James Harry was amazing.

We went to the 1931 Gallery Bistro upstairs from the art gallery for lunch. I had the avocado toast and Rachael had eggs Benedict with halloumi.

After lunch we went to our appointment at the Contemporary Art Gallery.

Since we were still wandering downtown — a town day, after all this pandemic time! — we went back up to Indigo Books on Robson so Rachael could get a new journal.

Then a final stop at Golden Age Collectables on Granville. Lots of people browsing, including us, so we thought we should buy something. I bought Crossover, Vol. 1: Kids Love Chains.

Sunday, June 6th, 2021

Today has been fully overcast and rainy, including some absolute downpour moments. When the rain slowed to a light sprinkle, we walked over to Laughing Bean for a cappuccino and an oat milk chai.

We got hit by downpours at one point, and we luckily had a covered spot on a bench outside to wait out several more downpours.

Rachael remembered that there was a sale on used / bargain books at Iron Dog Books around the corner, so we had a nice time browsing.

I ended up with a couple of new cookbooks, Heritage — covering “America’s heritage foods” — and a slim book called Classic African.

A couple of other cookbooks looked interesting, but that I didn’t buy:

A Facsimile of the First Edition of The Joy of Cooking — the original 1931 first edition of The Joy of Cooking, re-printed in a small hardcover.

On Food and Cooking — looked really interesting, but it’s the kind of process, techniques, and basics that I’d love to have electronically in a “wiki book”.

Coming back home, we had a quick lunch. Rachael finished up the leftovers we had the other night from Haru Korean Kitchen, and I made a quick ramen soup out of Amano Organic Aka Miso, a carrot, and Lanzhou Noodles Ramen.
