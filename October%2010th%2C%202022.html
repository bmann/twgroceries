Now to research duck tips for our meal tonight.

Martha Stewart Roast Duck 101

Mainly covers cutting off excess fat, cross hatch cutting into the fat to keep things crispy.

  • Preheat oven to 425 degrees
  • Roast breast side up 50 minutes
  • Flip and roast 50 minutes
  • Final flip, again breast side up, for 50 minutes, until duck registers 165 degrees

A word about the varieties of duck most commonly available: Muscovy very often has a strong flavor that tends toward gamy, while the Pekin, or Long Island, is milder.

Ordered a take out lunch from Green Lemongrass - beef pho, chicken pho, and some pork spring rolls.

Covered the duck in paprika and trimmed a bunch of fat and the wing tips.

Put the wing tips and giblets in some water plus chopped up apples, some carrots, some fresh Thai basil (leftover from pho!).

Turns out I had a Portuguese Casal Garcia Vinho Verde here at home that I don’t remember buying. I put it in the fridge and had some with dinner and some went into the stock.

I put apples and dried apricots into the duck cavity but I don’t think it added much.

The duck was excellently crispy but a little dry. I think it only need 2x 50min or maybe 30min per flip because it was smaller than the recipe OR because I put the heat higher because the oven doesn’t close with the sheet pan.

I sliced new potatoes in half and put them cut side down in a pan with duck fat and roasted.

The brussel sprouts I sautéed with shallots in some oil, then added a mixture of yuzu peels, mustard, and rice vinegar and then put in the oven to finish.

Rachael had two cake slices for dessert, both from Livia - one with rhubarb and one with plum.
