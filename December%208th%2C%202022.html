I've got a backlog of cooking things to log!

Yesterday we made pizza.

December 7th, 2022

We get frozen pizza dough from Bosa, and picked up some of the "best canned tomatoes in the world" – according to The Pie Shoppe, who currently make the best pizza in Vancouver :)

I used the Bianco DiNapoli Crushed Tomatoes. I still have a can of the Bianco DiNapoli Whole Peeled Tomatoes.

I added dried basil, dried oregano, dried garlic, some salt, some black pepper, a little bit of olive oil, and a splash of red wine vinegar to make the sauce.

Mozzarella from Choices, had some aged gouda in there, pepper salami, yellow bell peppers, and mushrooms.

Today, I used the leftover tomatoes to make spaghetti for lunch.

For dinner, Skipper Otto Albacore Tuna Loin with rice and cabbage.

I put a clove of sliced garlic in with the rice and made it with coconut oil as we usually do. We pretty much always have a giant bag of basmati rice. Which reminds me that I've been meaning to write up How to cook stove top rice.

The tuna loin was frozen. I put grapeseed and sesame oil in a stainless steel pan, and cut up a clove of garlic and a knob of peeled ginger to saute, then added the tuna loin and cooked on all three sides.

Then I poured in a mixture of soy sauce and rice vinegar and put it in the oven.

For cabbage, I took some Szechuan peppercorns and some oil in a cast iron pan, and then rough cut 2" thick slices of cabbage layered down, and sprinkled it all with Daisho yuzu salt.
