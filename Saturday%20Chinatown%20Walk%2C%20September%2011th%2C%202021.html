Saturday Chinatown Walk, September 11th, 2021

Boris Mann 11th September 2021 at 10:29pm
Journal

There is a Light Up Chinatown festival going on today and tomorrow, so we walked there to check things out.

We ended up not really going to anything. They had a stage setup and Columbia Street blocked off with food trucks, including Tokyo Katsu Sand and Takenaka.

But we went to our second visit to Kouign Cafe.

Rachael had the Golden Fog drink again, and I tried the Juice Box, both cold and with condensed milk added of course.

For a snack, I had the Croissmos and Rachael had the Purple Rain

Croissmos

brown butter black sesame banana bread with dark chocolate chips baked inside a flakey croissant, chrysanthemum honey and black sesame glaze, topped with cinnamon streusel

Purple Rain

gently warmed coconut vanilla brown butter bread pudding with shredded coconut, house-made Ube Jam, and 24K gold flakes

Both of those were pretty sweet, and we still needed a proper lunch. We wandered into International Village Mall.

Turns out Bara Caribbean is open earlier these days.

We got two orders of Doubles. Rachael's mild, mine extra spicy, plus a side of rice & beans and some fried plaintains. And a Ting to drink!

Very tasty once again after having it for the first time last Tuesday. Been thinking I should see if Fernando wants to check it out.

After lunch, we browsed through Raincity Games on the ground floor.

One more stop for Rachael to visit the Vancouver Pen Shop and we headed home.
