I took out the turkey wings and snipped off the tips for stock, and separated the drumstick from the wing.

Duck neck and giblets went into the stock, and I trimmed a lot of the fat.

I zested 3 oranges and combined the zest with salt and rubbed the duck all over. A few cloves of garlic and a bundle of fresh thyme in the body cavity.

My big half sheet pan doesn’t quite fit into our oven, but with its raised grate, it’s the best thing I have for crisp roasting.

One hour at 370F breast side down, then flipped and another hour and 15 or so and the duck came out nice and crispy.

I had leftover mashed potatoes, so I only had gravy and Brussel sprouts to make as sides. I made the sprouts with bacon ends and onions and a little duck fat, then added Dijon mustard, some yuzu syrup, and sherry vinegar, plus a little duck stock.
